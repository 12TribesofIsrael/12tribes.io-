Joseph, son of Jacob, bore two sons during his time in Eaypt (Gen. 41:50-52: Gen. 16.20), These two sons received the blessing of Reuben (Chrom. 51-2), which in turn caused them to be adopted as one of the son of Jacob (Gen. 48th chap), Joseph was prophesied to be a fruitful bough (Gen. 49'22-26), fulfilled through Ephraim and Manasseh who are known for having many children. A custom of both Ephraim and Manasseh is to extend their homes so that their children and grandchildren are able to live together under one roof. This is what Jacob identified as his "branches run over the wall'. The archers who grieved the children of Joseph are Spaniards and conquistadors who through genocide wiped out a large portion of Ephraim's population. Ephraim and Manasseh, like many of their brethren, blessed with fertile, tropical land (Deut. 23:13-17).
